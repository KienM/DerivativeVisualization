Visually Intuiting the Derivative

First, what is the derivative? It is the rate of change for a function. Visually, it is the slope of the line tangent to the curve - a rate of output with respect to its input.

Second, why does it matter? It matters, because in the world, things sprout and shrivel and change, and the rate at which this happens matters. Sometimes you'll be interested in how quickly, or sometimes how slowly something happens. Rates of change are important.
